# Product Requirements Document (PRD) — Mudarris

**Product Type:** Educational SaaS (Islamic & Qur’anic schools)  
**Phase:** Planning & MVP Definition  
**Status:** Conceptual / Pre-development

---

## 1. Overview

Mudarris is a lightweight Educational SaaS concept designed to introduce structure, accountability, and visibility into Qur’an education while respecting traditional teaching methods.

This PRD outlines the **initial MVP requirements** based on planning assumptions and is intended to guide future validation and development.

---

## 2. Objectives

- Enable teachers to log daily Qur’an lessons efficiently  
- Provide parents with passive visibility into student progress  
- Help students build consistency through lesson history  
- Minimize administrative overhead for teachers  

---

## 3. In Scope (MVP)

### 3.1 User & Classroom Management
- Teachers can create classes/groups  
- Teachers can add students to a class  
- Parents are linked to a student profile  
- Students (13+) may access their own profile  

---

### 3.2 Daily Lesson Planner
Teachers (or self-managed students) can log:
- New memorization (Hifz)  
- Review (Murājaʿah)  
- Reading practice (Tilāwah)  
- Optional notes (e.g., tajwīd feedback)

Entries are stored per student and date.

---

### 3.3 Lesson History & Progress Tracking
- Timeline view of previous lessons  
- Lessons can be marked as completed  
- Teachers can edit or update entries  

---

### 3.4 Parent Visibility
- Parents can view lesson history and daily updates  
- Progress updates occur automatically when lessons are logged  
- No parent editing permissions  

---

### 3.5 Messaging & Announcements
- Teachers can send:
  - Notes to individual parents  
  - Class-wide announcements  
- Text-based communication only  

---

### 3.6 Classroom Groups
- Each class represents a group of students with one teacher  
- Older students can view their group context  
- Optional teacher-only group notes  

---

### 3.7 Basic Qur’an Viewer (Limited)
- Scrollable Qur’an text or image view  
- Ability to highlight assigned āyāt  
- No audio, tafsīr, or advanced search  

---

## 4. Out of Scope (Post-MVP)

The following features are intentionally excluded from the MVP:
- Audio reciter libraries  
- Gamification (points, leaderboards, rewards)  
- Advanced Qur’an search  
- PDF or document exports  
- Quizzes or formal assessments  

These may be evaluated in later phases.

---

## 5. Non-Functional Requirements

- **Usability:** Minimal setup and low learning curve  
- **Performance:** Fast lesson logging and retrieval  
- **Privacy:** Role-based access (teacher, parent, student)  
- **Reliability:** Lesson data should persist accurately  

---

## 6. Assumptions & Constraints

- Teachers prefer simplicity over feature richness  
- Internet access may be inconsistent in some regions  
- Adoption depends on minimal disruption to teaching workflow  

All assumptions require validation before development.

---

## 7. Open Questions

- What level of student self-management is appropriate by age?  
- How much historical data do teachers want to review regularly?  
- What notification frequency works best for parents?  

These questions will guide future user research.
