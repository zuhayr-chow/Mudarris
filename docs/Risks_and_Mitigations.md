# Risks & Mitigations — Mudarris

**Product Type:** Educational SaaS (Islamic & Qur’anic schools)  
**Phase:** Planning & MVP Definition

This document identifies key risks associated with Mudarris and outlines mitigation strategies appropriate for Islamic and Qur’anic education environments.

---

## Risk 1: Low Teacher Adoption

**Description:**  
Teachers may resist adopting a new tool if it feels like additional administrative work.

**Impact:**  
- Inconsistent usage  
- Incomplete lesson data  
- Reduced platform value  

**Mitigation Strategies:**  
- Keep lesson logging minimal and fast  
- Align workflows with existing teaching practices  
- Avoid mandatory fields unless necessary  
- Validate usability early with real teachers  

---

## Risk 2: Increased Administrative Burden

**Description:**  
If not designed carefully, Mudarris could unintentionally add overhead to teachers.

**Impact:**  
- Teacher frustration  
- Reduced teaching focus  
- Early abandonment  

**Mitigation Strategies:**  
- Prioritize teacher-first workflows  
- Limit notifications and alerts  
- Default to optional features rather than required steps  

---

## Risk 3: Data Privacy & Trust Concerns

**Description:**  
Islamic schools and families may be cautious about how student data is stored and accessed.

**Impact:**  
- Reduced adoption  
- Loss of trust  
- Institutional pushback  

**Mitigation Strategies:**  
- Implement strict role-based access controls  
- Limit data visibility to relevant parties only  
- Avoid unnecessary data collection  
- Follow confidentiality practices commonly used in masjid-based institutions  

---

## Risk 4: Misuse or Over-Reliance on Metrics

**Description:**  
Progress indicators may be misinterpreted as performance rankings.

**Impact:**  
- Student pressure  
- Misalignment with spiritual learning goals  

**Mitigation Strategies:**  
- Avoid leaderboards or comparative scoring  
- Frame metrics as personal progress indicators  
- Emphasize qualitative feedback over numerical scores  

---

## Risk 5: Cultural or Pedagogical Misalignment

**Description:**  
Generic EdTech patterns may conflict with traditional Qur’anic teaching methods.

**Impact:**  
- Rejection by teachers  
- Reduced instructional effectiveness  

**Mitigation Strategies:**  
- Design around Hifz, Murājaʿah, and Tilāwah workflows  
- Avoid automated religious judgments  
- Preserve teacher authority and discretion  

---

## Risk 6: Premature Feature Expansion

**Description:**  
Adding advanced features too early could complicate the product and dilute its core value.

**Impact:**  
- Reduced usability  
- Slower adoption  
- Higher maintenance burden  

**Mitigation Strategies:**  
- Validate each feature before expansion  
- Maintain a narrow MVP scope  
- Treat roadmap phases as flexible, not guaranteed  

---

## Summary

Mudarris is designed with a **cautious, respectful, and validation-driven approach**.  
By proactively identifying risks and defining mitigations, the product aims to support Qur’anic education responsibly while maintaining trust with teachers, parents, and institutions.

---

## Disclaimer

This risk analysis reflects **planning-phase assumptions** and will be revisited as the product evolves and feedback is collected.
