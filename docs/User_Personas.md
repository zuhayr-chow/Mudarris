# User Personas — Mudarris

Mudarris is designed around three primary user groups within Islamic and Qur’anic education. Each persona reflects real-world roles, goals, and constraints observed in madāris, masjid programs, and online Qur’an institutes.

---

## Persona 1: Qur’an Teacher (Mudarris)

**Role:** Primary user  
**Context:** Teaches multiple students across varying levels (Hifz, Murājaʿah, Tilāwah)

### Goals
- Track daily lessons accurately and consistently  
- Reduce mental load when managing multiple students  
- Maintain quality instruction without added admin work  

### Pain Points
- Reliance on memory or scattered notes  
- Repeating updates to parents individually  
- Difficulty reviewing past lesson history quickly  

### Needs
- Simple daily lesson logging  
- Clear lesson history timeline  
- Minimal clicks and setup  

### Success Looks Like
- Lessons are logged quickly after class  
- Parents are informed automatically  
- Teaching time stays focused on instruction, not administration  

---

## Persona 2: Parent / Guardian

**Role:** Secondary user  
**Context:** Responsible for a child enrolled in Qur’an classes

### Goals
- Understand their child’s consistency and progress  
- Support learning at home  
- Stay informed without constant follow-ups  

### Pain Points
- Limited visibility into daily lessons  
- Uncertainty about whether assignments are completed  
- Hesitation to frequently message the teacher  

### Needs
- Passive visibility into lesson progress  
- Simple, easy-to-understand updates  
- Confidence that learning is on track  

### Success Looks Like
- Can quickly see recent lessons and assignments  
- Feels informed without interrupting the teacher  
- Trusts the learning process  

---

## Persona 3: Student (Age 13+)

**Role:** Supporting user  
**Context:** Self-managed or semi-independent learner

### Goals
- Know exactly what was assigned  
- Track personal progress  
- Stay consistent and accountable  

### Pain Points
- Forgetting assignments  
- No clear record of past lessons  
- Difficulty measuring progress over time  

### Needs
- Clear lesson records  
- Simple progress indicators  
- Ability to mark lessons as completed  

### Success Looks Like
- Can review past lessons independently  
- Feels motivated through visible progress  
- Builds consistent study habits  

---

## Persona Relationships

Mudarris supports a **connected ecosystem**:
- Teachers manage lessons and classrooms  
- Parents view progress without manual updates  
- Students engage with their own learning history  

Each persona benefits from shared visibility while maintaining appropriate access boundaries.

---

## Persona Assumptions

These personas are based on:
- Observed workflows in Islamic and Qur’anic education  
- Common challenges in online and masjid-based programs  
- Planning-phase assumptions to be validated during user feedback
